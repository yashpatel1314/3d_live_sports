# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual NBA fans at home who can't stream a game — watching on a phone or laptop. Their context: they care about a specific game, want to know the score in real time, and want to feel the excitement of big plays without needing a full broadcast. The primary job: stay emotionally connected to the game through a 3D visualization of key moments, not just a score ticker.

## Product Purpose

3D Live Sports makes NBA games watchable without a stream. It fetches live play-by-play data and renders key moments — dunks, 3-pointers, fadeaways — as real-time 3D animations on a full basketball court. Success: the user opens the app for a game they care about, sees a big play animation happen, and feels like they didn't miss it.

## Brand Personality

Electric · Immersive · Precise

Voice: confident and athletic, never frenetic. Copy is minimal — the 3D court does the talking. When text speaks, it's sharp and direct.

Emotional goal: the feeling of sitting courtside, watching a live broadcast, compressed into a hybrid app + broadcast experience.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark SaaS dashboard (blue-gray tokens, sidebar nav, admin-panel feel — this should feel like a venue, not a tool)
- Fantasy sports / gambling apps (DraftKings, FanDuel — cluttered, anxiety-driven, dark-green + gold overload)
- Plain score ticker (ESPN BottomLine — pure data, zero drama or personality)
- WebGL showcase overload (a 3D scene so demanding it hides the actual game data)

## Design Principles

1. **The court is the hero.** The 3D scene is the product. Every UI element frames it — it never competes.
2. **Broadcast, not dashboard.** Spacing, weight, and component language should feel like something a professional broadcast team built, not assembled from a design-system template.
3. **Drama at the right moment.** Play announcements and score updates are the emotional peaks — treat them accordingly. Restraint everywhere else earns the drama when it arrives.
4. **Information hierarchy over decoration.** Every visible element earns its place by serving the fan in the moment. If it doesn't serve, cut it.
5. **Premium restraint.** Apple TV+ sports overlays use clean space and tight type, not visual noise. Restraint is confidence here.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort: WCAG AA contrast targets on body text and key interactive elements, keyboard navigation on all links and controls, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations (crossfade fallback).
